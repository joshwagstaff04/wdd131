About

Zion National Park sits in southwest Utah and is famous for towering red and orange sandstone cliffs, narrow slot canyons, and the Virgin River that carves through the valley. It became a national park in 1919 and continues to be one of the most visited parks in the United States.

Popular hikes include Angels Landing and The Narrows. The park’s scenery changes with the seasons—wildflowers and high water in spring, warm and dry summers, bright fall colors, and crisp winter air with occasional snow on the cliffs.

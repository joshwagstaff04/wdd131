Budgeting Tips Sources

The 12 budgeting tips on the Budgeting Tips page are based on widely-accepted personal finance principles and methodologies from the following sources:

  • The 50/30/20 Rule
    A budgeting framework popularized by Elizabeth Warren and Amelia Warren Tyagi in their book "All Your Worth: The Ultimate Lifetime Money Plan."
    Referenced in: Khan Academy, Federal Reserve Consumer Resources, and YNAB methodology.
  • Emergency Fund Principles
    Guidelines for building emergency funds (3-6 months of living expenses) are recommended by the Federal Reserve and personal finance experts including Dave Ramsey and Suze Orman.
  • Meal Planning & Budget Management
    Practical tips for reducing food expenses and variable spending are drawn from consumer financial education resources and USDA budget guidelines.
  • Lifestyle Inflation & Savings Strategies
    Concepts of "paying yourself first" and avoiding lifestyle inflation are core principles found in "Your Money or Your Life" and personal finance best practices.

Methodology References

  • 50/30/20 Budgeting Rule
    A widely-used budgeting framework that allocates 50% to needs, 30% to wants, and 20% to savings and debt repayment.
  • Zero-Based Budgeting
    A budgeting method where every dollar is assigned a purpose before spending occurs.
  • Emergency Fund Principles
    Financial guidance recommending 3-6 months of living expenses in readily accessible savings.
